5 Important Things about Skype for Business

Nabeel Shoro June 20, 2015 Apps Review

As we know that Microsoft launch the service Skype for Business, which replaces Microsoft Lync. However, despite the similarities, Skype and Skype for Business are practically different applications, which require separate accounts, but at first the name of the new service has misled many people who think of it as an updated version of Skype.

Now everything fell into place, but if you are still confused and not sure what the similarities and differences between Skype and Skype for Business, then the information in this article will be helpful. After reading, you'll know some important information about Skype for Business.

There is no limit of conversation between people at a time

Image Credit: Wikimedia

Did you know that standard Skype client currently limits conferences up to 25 people? But Skype for Business has raised this limit to 250, this makes much more suitable for large presentation. Participants of the meeting in Skype or Skype for business are not necessarily to join them with a client Skype, this can be done from a fixed or mobile phone, but it's an additional cost.

Easy integration with other Microsoft Office applications

Skype for Business integrates directly into Microsoft Office 365. In essence, this means that the user can use Outlook directly with chat, video calls, conference calls and many other features offered by Skype for Business.

For example, simply click a contact to start a conversation with him or make an appointment. The advantages of this integration, you can use in other applications Office 365. In particular, start the conference and allow other users to view your screen without leaving PowerPoint or Excel.

Skype for Business has tight Security system

All traffic through Skype for business is encrypted using the algorithm AES. However, Skype for Business uses stronger authentication methods that provide more control over your account and access to it.

Skype for business provides a Conference hall

In cases where two remote offices want to chat with each other through video, Skype for Business is a very good choice for this purpose. New Feature Skype conference hall system can be used with Skype cameras, monitors and audio equipment from Polycom, while Microsoft Surface Hub can be used as a digital online whiteboard. Skype for Business also integrates with a number of PBX-enterprise-class systems, thus providing a convenient way of communication through VOIP-phone.

Price of Skype for Business

Skype for Business is a paid service, which has two main plans.

Plan 1" will cost you $ 100 per user per month (annual subscription), but it covers only the basic functions. In fact plane 1" will give you very little in comparison with the standard Skype. But you can get much more, including high-definition video in the group The Conference may join meetings via a web browser and desktop sharing if you select the plan 1.

Plane 2" for 275$ per month, you can get much more as compare plane 1. So if you want to use skype for you business, above description helps you in choosing skype for business.
