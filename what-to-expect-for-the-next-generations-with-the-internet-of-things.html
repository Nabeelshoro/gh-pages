What to Expect For the Next Generations With the 'Internet of Things'

Nabeel Shoro July 28, 2015 Technology Overview

Nowadays it is impossible to imagine living without internet access or without a smartphone. The new generations, already have access to electronic devices connected and are more dependent on technology in day-to-day, is to watch online content, maintaining interpersonal relationships or perform everyday activities like watching hours in a digital device.

What to expect for the next generations with the 'Internet of Things', where life will mold around the technologies offered. We are living today a technological revolution that will connect all our routine to the World Wide Web.

In technology sectors, every day, new technologies and prototypes are designed, developed and tested. Companies are preparing to meet future demand where any innovation must already be connected to the internet and independent functions of the human command, these services are born to make life easier.

Let’s imagine our home, our car, smartphone, and all its connected devices. These gadgets already has the best way to get away from transit points. When you arrive at home you can turn on the lights, through your smartphone. To come into your house simply position your eyes in front of an optical reader to access inside. Turn on TV or radio through voice command and choose the music or programming that would. It looks very futuristic? Many of them already exist but wait other innovations about to come.

Also read: - Cloud Technology – Cloud Computing for a Business

The Internet of Things brings meaning to the concept of ubiquitous connectivity to businesses, governments, and consumers to management, monitoring and analysis. From a marketing point of view, the 'Internet of Things' consists of several interconnected technologies and for this to work companies need to create an ecosystem to enable new business models.

Intelligent product opportunities are endless: cars without driver using GPS, payments approach using smartphone or smartwatch (Wearable Devices), smart objects as lamps, refrigerators, ovens and even toothbrush brushing that monitors and provides feedback on the pressure angle and duration of brushing. Technologies to help doctors to collect patient information remotely and be warned whenever there is a problem.

The 'Internet of Things' comes to revolutionize the world we live in; making everything that is connected to communicate with each other, following the concept of data centers and clouds. Scanning, simplifying and facilitating all consumer demands to come. There are so many application possibilities it is impossible to predict what the limit is?
