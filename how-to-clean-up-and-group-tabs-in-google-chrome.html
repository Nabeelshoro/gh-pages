How to clean up and group tabs in Google Chrome

Nabeel Shoro March 17, 2020 How To

Many of us have dozens of tabs open in Chrome at the same time. To put things in order, we install various extensions or even switch to other browsers - Today The Tricks Peak author brings you a way of clean up your Google Chrome browser and how to combined tabs into groups.

There is no need for additional tools. Google Chrome already can sort tabs by category. It’s just hidden in the settings.

Here's how to activate this feature. Type in the address bar chrome://flags/ and press Enter. In the opened settings in the search, type Tab Groups.

Switch the Tab Groups option that appears to Enabled, then click the Relaunch button at the bottom. The browser will restart.

Now right-click on any tab and click “Add to New Group”. The tab will be colored, a colored circle will appear next to it.

You can click on it and change the hue of the group of tabs, and also give it a name.

When you want to assign a tab to an already created group, right-click it and select "Add to an existing group." Or just drag the item with the mouse.

You can create any number of groups, but the number of colors is limited - only eight. However, this is enough to restore order.
