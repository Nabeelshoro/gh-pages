Seven Basic Tips | Onpage SEO

Nabeel Shoro December 11, 2014 SEO

Onpage SEO is the technique of optimizing a website or blog to be favored by search engines.

Onpage called because that is optimized the website itself, not by way of external websites like finding, back links or insert articles to social book-marking.

The search engine will look for pages that best match the search terms entered by the user.

That is why, it is important to target keywords that are searched at least more than 1000 times a month.

So it would be useless to optimize a page containing keywords with little or no demand.

Here are seven Onpage SEO tips that you can use to ensure that the search engines pay attention to your website for a particular search term.

1. Buy a domain name corresponding to the phrases or keywords that you like

For example, if the keywords that you shoot are 'SEO' the ideal domain name you are seotips dot com.

If you can not get the exact same name, try using hyphens or add a word as a suffix like seotoptips dot com. Read my previous article, what to write about a blog or website.

You can add a prefix word but word endings tend to rank better in the search engines.

2. Make sure the keywords appear in the URL of the page

This has been handled if the domain name already contains the keyword.Â But if not, you can do Onpage SEO on a sub-page.Â So for example, you will have a URL like seotips dot com/onpage-seo-tips.

3. Make sure the keyword appears in the name of the website and the keywords meta tag.

If you do not know what this means, you need to make sure that your web developer to understand about this. Keyword tags may not be as important as the first but still fit for use as part of your Onpage SEO strategy.

4. Enter keywords into the headings (titles and subtitles of the article)

If you can put keywords into the various levels of headings such as heading 1, heading 2 etc., it will attract the attention of search engines.

Ideally, place the keyword at least to heading 1, heading 2 and heading 3.

5. Create keyword prominence

Create keyword prominence by making it bold, italic, or underline.Â Search engines consider words with special format as something important.

6. If you have an image on a web page, enter a keyword into the "alt" tags

If you do not have a picture, you may want to find images that are relevant to your article.Â Not only can you use it for Onpage SEO but also can add to the attractiveness of the article for the reader.

7. Make the keyword appears in the first sentence and the last sentence in your article

Search engines often pay more attention to the beginning and end of the article content.Â It is important to increase your Onpage SEO.
